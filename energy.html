Energy

Large AI models require substantial energy during training, processing massive datasets across specialized processors for extended periods.

After training, inference continues to consume energy each time users submit prompts, generate content, or run model-based tools.

AI workloads are concentrated in large data centers that require stable, high-capacity electricity and continuous operation.

Factors Influencing AI Energy Impact

Factor What It Affects Environmental Implication
Grid Carbon Intensity Emissions per unit of electricity Fossil-fuel grids increase AI-related carbon output
Hardware Efficiency Energy required per computation Efficient processors reduce electricity demand
Cooling Design Total facility power load Inefficient cooling increases system-wide energy use
Renewable Energy Sourcing Energy origin Renewable procurement lowers carbon intensity
